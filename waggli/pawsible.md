Pet owners worldwide face an overwhelming reality: managing pet care is fragmented, time-consuming, and unnecessarily stressful. Despite the deep bond between pets and their families (71% consider pets family members), there is no unified platform that brings together health management, social connection, veterinary coordination, and essential services.

Today's reality for pet owners globally:

Scattered Records, Dangerous Care Gaps
Pet health records exist across paper documents, multiple vet systems, and disconnected apps. When owners switch vets, travel internationally, or face emergencies, critical health information gets lost‚Äîcreating real risks: missed vaccinations, medication conflicts, incomplete histories during emergencies.

Manual Data Entry Kills Engagement
Existing pet health apps require owners to manually type every vet visit, vaccination, and medication. This friction leads to 80%+ abandonment rates. Pet owners start with good intentions but stop tracking within weeks.

No Single Source of Truth
Pet owners currently juggle:

Paper vaccination cards and scattered documents
3-5 different apps for different services
Separate lost pet recovery systems
No connection between health data and services
No intelligent guidance or recommendations
Vets Lack Complete Patient History
Veterinarians spend 5-10 minutes per appointment gathering health history verbally. When pets see emergency vets or specialists, they start from zero‚Äîpotentially dangerous in emergencies.

No Intelligence in Pet Care
Current solutions are passive record-keepers. They don't proactively alert owners to due vaccinations, recommend breed-specific screenings, or connect health data to personalized care recommendations.

Disconnected Pet Community
Pet owners lack a unified platform to connect with their local pet community‚Äîfinding blood donors in emergencies, discovering shelter pets, or coordinating with trusted service providers.

High Stress, Low Support
Pet ownership should be joyful, but the mental load of tracking health, finding services, coordinating care, and worrying about emergencies creates unnecessary stress for millions of pet owners.
